Introduction

Welcome to the virtual exhibition of Exploring the Final Frontier: The Intersection of Culture and Science. The gallery is a collection of 3D models that are related to the theme of humanity's quest for scientific advancement and the exploration of the cosmos. The objects chosen in this showcase include the International Space Station (ISS), the Mars Exploration Rover (MER), and the Hubble Telescope. The purpose of this exhibit is not too explore, in-depth, the history and makings of these technologies. Rather, it is to understand these objects as expressing unique traits of human nature manifested through these technologies.

As humanity ventures into space, our understanding of our place in the universe further unravels, and the degree to which we make progress on these projects (or not) expresses the collective values of the human race. Institutions, individuals, and cultural practices support the development and use of space technology. Scientific inquiry, exploration, and collaboration are values at the heart of these projects. The development and funding of these projects are tied to the political and economic interests of the countries involved. The production of these projects embody and shape our shared cultural history and humanity's vision of the future.

The Hubble Telescope has revolutionized our understanding of the cosmos. Scientists have captured stunning images of galaxies, nebulas, and other celestial objects, which has helped us appreciate the vastness of the universe and expanded our understanding of its origins and evolution. It reflects how little we know about the space we occupy in existence, how much there is to know, and how much we don't know about what we don't know.

The International Space Station (ISS) is a symbol of international collaboration that represents the peaceful coexistences of nation states. It is a symbol of the potential of humanity to work together to achieve great things. The Mars Exploration Rover represents our desire to explore and understand our neighbouring planet. Is there, or was there, life on Mars? Recent findings suggest that there has been.


If this exhibition were in a physical space, Virtual Reality (VR) headsets would be provided to allow visitors to explore every detail of the object. Instead, this website allows visitors to point, drag, and click through their computer monitors to resemble a VR experience. I find this in keeping, and in celebration of, the advancements of technology and its utility in our everyday lives. in the 21st century, technology is inextricably linked to human societies.

Physics, computer science, and engineering now supports and enables nearly every aspect of human societies. Our relationships to technology can be clarified through the greatest feats of the scientific enterprise. Through these objects, we invite you to explore the ways in which culture shapes our understanding of space exploration and vice versa. I hope that this exhibition will inspire you to reflect on the relationship between science and our collective cultural identity that seems to be expanding into the depths of space.

Hubble Space Telescope

Developed jointly by NASA and ESA Launched April 24, 1990, 11:34 UT0 at the Kennedy Space Center, Fla. (USA) Measures 13.2 meters long and 4.2 meters wide, with a mass of 11,110 kilograms
The Hubble Space Telescope is made up of mirrors, cameras, and scientific equipment that allows us to see as far as 13.2 billion light years away. It has revolutionized our understanding of the universe, and has captured stunning images of galaxies, nebulas, and other celestial objects. It has also helped us understand the origins and evolution of the universe.
The main body holds the main instruments that operate the Hubble. The current instruments on Hubble were installed in 2009. The Wide Field Camera 3 (WFC3) allows for the resolution of 0.1 arc seconds, which is 10 times better than the previous WFC2 1. The unfathomable distance it can reach have challenged our cultural beliefs about humanity's significance in the cosmos. This technology has led to further innovations in medicine, climate science 2 , and GPS technology. LASIK eye surgery 3 was invented, provided hard evidence of our thinning ozone layer, and has allowed us to navigate to the nearest grocery store.
It's Solar panels (two rectangles attached to the main body) are a careful innovation that produce enough power for all of Hubble's instruments to operate. Its Communication antennas (circular objects appended to its main body) allow digital images and spectra to be transmitted to Earth.
Physicists have used data collected from Hubble to contribute to the understanding of the origins of the universe. There has always been pushback on these findings that go against the creation myths of various cultures. Hubble continues to spark debates on the subject of existentialism, subjects which inform and challenge our sense of self.

International Space Station

Developed jointly by NASA, Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), and CSA (Canada) Assembly began in 1998 and was completed in 2011 Measures 109 meters wide and 73 meters long, with a mass of 419,455 kilograms
The International Space Station (ISS) is a significant token of global cooperation where collective values of collaboration, scientific advancement, and exploration aligns humanity.
The ISS is used for a wide range of scientific research, including studies of human physiology in space, materials science, and Earth observation.
The main body includes living quarters, laboratories, and exercise facilities for the crew. The large solar panels on each side of the body were designed and manufactured by different companies around the world, including Boeing (USA), Alenia Space (Europe), and Mitsubishi Electric (Japan). The design and construction of these panels reflect national priorities towards space exploration and scientific research. Research on the ISS has had many benefits for humanity, including advancing medical scanning technology, new drugs to treat Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD), and filtration technologies that helped in the fight against COVID-19, among other benefits 4.
What the ISS makes entirely clear is the importance of international cooperation to bring about feats of human achievement that cannot be accomplished alone. The social reality that is constructed by this international engagement expresses how engagement in scientific research creates global communities.

Mars Exploration Rover (Opportunity)

Developed by NASA Launched in 2003 and landed on Mars in January 2004 Measures 1.5 meters long, 2.3 meters wide, and 1.5 meters tall, with a weight of 180 kilograms
The Mars Exploration Rover mission received global attention. It was humanity's first exploration of another planet. It occupied over 11 years on the planet before shutting down. It revealed evidence of liquid water on Mars, which is a key ingredient for life. It also discovered evidence of ancient Martian lakes and rivers, which is a significant step in understanding the history of the planet5.
Mars Opportunity Rover is almost animal-like in appearance, with panoramic cameras for eyes, robotic appendages with claws for arms and wheels for legs. It's body consists of solar arrays to support its functionality and antennas to communicate data back to Earth. The rover's mission was to find evidence of past life on Mars. The design of Opportunity entirely reflects an era of humanity's technological advancement. If Opportunity were discovered by future races, or aliens, they would find its design entirely peculiar and belonging to a species or era not of its own. It's design is an expression of a human being's sensory perceptions. It has eyes to see, appendages to touch and traverse the ground it stands on, ways to communicate, audio sensors to hear, and a body to house its internal components.
The Rover also represents humanity's quest to expand its occupation of the cosmos to other planets. Is life on Mars habitable? Can we develop the technology to find out? The Opportunity Rover can be seen as an early attempt of humanity to answer these questions. What explorations of other worlds and the global attention these projects received reveals just how interested humanity is in discovering the cosmos.
